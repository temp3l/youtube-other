# Even Killers Can Lick

When the police entered the apartment, they found blood everywhere.

In the bathroom.

In the hallway.

Under the bed.

But the man who had spent the entire night inside the apartment was gone.

The only thing he left behind was a handwritten message.

Six words that would haunt the surviving woman for the rest of her life:

**“Even killers can lick your hand.”**

## The Woman and Her Dog

The young woman lived alone in a large apartment on the edge of the city.

The building was old. Wooden floors creaked, heating pipes knocked at night, and footsteps sometimes came from the apartment above even though nobody had lived there for months.

Still, she never felt completely alone.

She had Bruno.

Bruno was a large black mixed-breed dog with broad shoulders and a calm temperament. He rarely barked, but whenever somebody approached the front door, he stood in front of it and growled so deeply that visitors instinctively stepped back.

The woman trusted him completely.

He was more than a pet.

He was her alarm system.

Her family.

And the reason she felt safe at night.

She had a strange habit.

Whenever she lay in bed, she let one hand hang over the edge. Bruno usually slept directly underneath.

As soon as he licked her fingers, she knew he was there.

Then she could close her eyes.

Everything was fine.

Or so she had always believed.

## Someone Is Watching

One cold Thursday evening, the woman came home later than usual.

Heavy rain blurred the streetlights into yellow stains across the wet windows.

Before she reached the building, she noticed a man standing across the road.

He wore a dark jacket and kept his hands in his pockets. She could not make out his face.

As she approached the entrance, his eyes followed her.

She told herself he was waiting for someone.

But after closing the heavy front door behind her, she looked through the glass.

The man was still standing there.

Now he was staring directly at the building.

Directly at her.

She hurried upstairs.

When she reached the third floor, she noticed that her apartment door was slightly open.

Only a narrow crack.

She stopped.

She had locked it that morning. She was certain.

“Bruno?”

No answer.

Normally, she would hear his paws crossing the wooden floor immediately.

This time, the apartment remained silent.

She slowly pushed the door open.

The hallway was dark.

Her hand searched for the light switch.

For a moment, she thought she saw something move at the far end.

When the light came on, nobody was there.

Then Bruno emerged from the living room.

He moved slowly, his head lowered.

“There you are.”

She knelt and stroked his fur.

The dog seemed nervous. His muscles were tense, and he kept looking toward the open bedroom door.

The woman checked every room.

The kitchen.

The living room.

The bathroom.

The bedroom.

Nothing was missing.

No window was open. No drawer had been searched.

Perhaps she had simply failed to close the door properly.

When she looked outside again, the man was gone.

## The First Drip

Shortly before midnight, the woman went to bed.

Bruno followed her into the bedroom but stopped at the door.

“Come on.”

The dog did not move.

He stared into the dark hallway and began to growl.

She switched on the hall light.

Nobody was there.

“You are making me nervous.”

Eventually, Bruno walked to the bed and disappeared underneath.

The woman lay down, pulled the blanket to her chin, and let her right hand hang over the edge.

For several seconds, nothing happened.

Then a warm tongue moved across her fingertips.

Once.

Slowly.

Reassuringly.

She smiled.

“Good night, Bruno.”

Soon she fell asleep.

In the middle of the night, her eyes opened.

The bedroom was dark.

The rain had stopped.

At first, she did not know what had awakened her.

Then she heard it.

**Drip.**

A pause.

**Drip.**

Silence.

**Drip.**

The sound came from the kitchen.

She remained still and listened.

She had probably left the faucet slightly open.

The woman reached her hand under the bed.

Almost immediately, Bruno licked her fingers.

She exhaled.

Everything was fine.

Still, the dripping would not let her sleep.

She got up and walked to the kitchen.

The faucet was dripping.

A heavy drop gathered beneath the metal, fell into the sink, and broke with a soft sound.

**Drip.**

She tightened the tap.

The sound stopped.

As she turned around, the kitchen door slowly moved shut.

She froze.

Perhaps it was a draft.

But no window was open.

For a moment, she thought she heard someone breathing on the other side.

“Bruno?”

No response.

She pulled the door open.

The hallway was empty.

She shook her head and returned to bed.

Under the blanket, she let her hand fall over the edge.

A tongue crossed her fingers.

Everything was fine.

## The Second Drip

About an hour later, she woke again.

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

This time, the sound was closer.

It came from the living room.

“That is impossible.”

She had closed the faucet.

Again, she lowered her hand.

Bruno licked her.

But now it felt different.

Slower.

The tongue remained against her skin for too long.

She pulled her hand away.

“Bruno?”

Something shifted beneath the bed.

A faint scraping sound.

Then silence.

The woman turned on the bedside lamp but did not bend down to look.

She did not know why.

Instead, she walked toward the living room.

The dripping grew louder.

A bottle of water lay open on the floor.

She could not remember leaving it there.

Water escaped through the loose cap and fell onto the wood.

She picked it up and closed it tightly.

Beside the bottle, she noticed wet marks.

They looked almost like shoeprints.

But they were blurred and vanished after a few steps.

They led from the living room toward the bedroom.

The woman looked down the hallway.

The bedroom door stood open.

Only darkness waited behind it.

“Bruno?”

Again, the dog did not answer.

She returned to the bed and carefully knelt down.

With one hand, she lifted the blanket hanging over the side.

It was too dark underneath to see anything.

“Come out.”

Nothing moved.

She reached into the darkness.

Her fingers touched fur.

The tension left her body.

“You idiot.”

Bruno was there after all.

She withdrew her hand, lay down, and switched off the light.

A few seconds later, something licked her fingers.

This time, she did not smile.

## Footsteps in the Hall

She could no longer sleep properly.

Again and again, she thought she heard footsteps.

Slow and careful.

One floorboard creaked in the hallway.

Then another.

She held her breath.

“Bruno.”

Something moved beneath the bed.

A tongue touched her hand again.

If Bruno was under the bed, nobody could be in the hallway.

Perhaps the wood was expanding.

Perhaps the sounds came from another apartment.

Then she heard the bathroom door click.

She knew she had left it open.

Now it slowly closed.

The woman sat up and reached for her phone.

The screen remained black.

The battery was dead.

That made no sense. It had been nearly full when she went to sleep.

She connected the charger.

Nothing happened.

The bedside lamp no longer worked either.

The apartment fell into complete darkness.

Only one sound remained.

**Drip.**

A pause.

**Drip.**

Then faster.

**Drip. Drip. Drip.**

The woman kept her hand beneath the bed.

A tongue brushed her fingers.

She could have remained there until morning.

But then she noticed a smell.

Metallic.

Warm.

Sweet.

A smell her mind did not immediately identify, but her body recognized as danger.

She pulled her hand back.

Her fingertips were wet.

A passing car sent light through the window for one second.

She looked at her hand.

A dark liquid covered her skin.

Not saliva.

Blood.

## The Bathroom

She jumped out of bed.

“Bruno!”

No answer came from the hallway.

The dripping in the bathroom grew louder.

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

With trembling legs, she approached the door.

Every instinct told her to leave the apartment.

But Bruno might be hurt.

She could not abandon him.

The hallway was cold.

The bathroom door stood open a few centimeters.

A thin line of light crossed the floor.

The bathroom light was on, even though the bedroom had lost power.

She pushed the door wider.

At first, everything looked normal.

The sink.

The mirror.

The white tiles.

The closed shower curtain.

Then a red drop fell from above and struck the edge of the bathtub.

**Drip.**

It moved slowly down the white surface.

The woman stared at the curtain.

A dark shape hung behind it.

“Bruno?”

The shape moved slightly.

Not because it was alive.

Because it was suspended.

She grabbed the curtain and pulled it aside.

Bruno hung from the shower fixture.

Someone had cut open his body and secured him with a cable. His black fur was soaked with blood.

Drops fell from his paws.

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

The woman could not make a sound.

Bruno was dead.

Not for minutes.

His body was already cold.

That meant he could not have been beneath the bed.

Not during the first dripping sound.

Not during the second.

And he could not have licked her hand.

## The Message

A small note was attached to Bruno's collar.

The paper was soaked in blood.

The woman did not want to touch it.

But she needed to know.

She unfolded it.

Only one sentence had been written:

**“Even killers can lick.”**

The woman screamed.

She dropped the paper and stumbled backward.

Then she heard something behind her.

A quiet creak from the bedroom.

Someone was crawling out from under her bed.

Slow footsteps crossed the wooden floor.

The man had been there all night.

While she slept.

While she walked through the apartment.

While she believed her dog was licking her hand.

The footsteps approached.

She locked the bathroom door and pressed both hands against it.

On the other side, everything became silent.

Then a fingernail slowly scratched down the wood.

A man's voice whispered:

“Everything is fine.”

Those were the exact words she said to Bruno every night.

## Beneath the Bed

The neighbors found her the following morning.

She had remained inside the locked bathroom, pressing her body against the door.

By the time police arrived, the intruder was gone.

A bedroom window stood open.

Bloody footprints crossed the floor beneath it.

But they did not lead outside.

They led from the window to the bed.

Under the mattress, investigators found food wrappers, empty water bottles, and a flashlight.

They also found hair.

Not only Bruno's.

Several strands belonged to the woman.

They had been arranged carefully beside one another.

The man may not have entered that evening.

He may have spent several nights inside the apartment.

Perhaps weeks.

Under her bed.

While she slept.

Later, the woman remembered that objects had moved during the previous days.

A cup appeared in the kitchen although she had left it in the living room.

A cabinet door stood open.

Food disappeared.

She had ignored every sign.

Because each night, she lowered her hand beneath the bed.

And each night, something licked her fingers.

She believed Bruno was there.

She believed everything was fine.

That was exactly what the man understood.

He did not need to overpower her.

He only needed to imitate the one signal that made her feel safe.

The woman moved away soon afterward.

She never entered the apartment again.

For the rest of her life, she slept only on mattresses placed directly on the floor.

The intruder was never identified.

And sometimes, when she woke at night, she thought she heard the sound again.

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

**Drip.**

Then she felt something warm touch her hand.

Even though there was no longer any space beneath her bed.
