# When the Candle Went Out, the Werewolf Returned

During the summer of 1988, an alarm sounded at an American military installation in Germany's Hunsrück region.

The security system detected movement near the outer fence.

At first, the soldiers assumed it was a wild boar or a deer.

Then they reached the area and saw a figure almost two meters tall.

It stood on two legs.

Its body was covered in dark fur.

And while the guard dogs whimpered and pulled away, the creature tried to climb the fence.

The soldiers aimed their weapons.

Before anyone fired, the figure disappeared into the forest.

Minutes later, it returned.

This time, it was already inside the base.

That same night, a candle had gone out inside a small roadside shrine near Morbach.

According to local legend, that candle must never be allowed to die.

Because as long as it burns, the Werewolf of Morbach remains in his grave.

## The Forests of the Hunsrück

Morbach lies in the Hunsrück, a landscape of hills and forests in western Germany.

Today, roads cross the region and wind turbines stand on its heights.

Centuries ago, the forests were denser.

The roads were poor.

And anybody traveling after dark could go for hours without meeting another person.

People feared more than wild animals.

They feared robbers.

Deserters.

Men who lived in the forest and attacked travelers.

One of those men was said to be Johannes.

Some versions give him another name. Some call him a soldier. Others describe him as a ruthless highwayman.

But every version agrees on one point.

Johannes was cruel.

He robbed travelers and killed anyone who resisted.

People began disappearing on the roads around Morbach.

Torn clothing and bloodstains were found.

So were deep tracks in the mud.

They did not resemble human footprints.

They looked like the paws of an enormous wolf.

## The Curse

According to the legend, Johannes had once served as a soldier in a foreign campaign.

During a raid, his unit attacked a small village.

The soldiers looted the homes.

Inside one house, they found a young woman.

She begged them to spare her family.

Johannes laughed.

He tore an amulet from her neck and pushed her to the ground.

Before he left, she cursed him.

“From this day forward, you will become the animal already living inside you.”

Johannes ignored her.

During the next full moon, his body changed.

His bones stretched.

Dark fur grew through his skin.

His hands became claws.

The man became something neither fully human nor fully animal.

At dawn, Johannes woke naked and covered in blood.

The remains of a fellow soldier lay beside him.

From then on, the story says, he hunted during every full moon.

Eventually, he returned to the Hunsrück.

The curse followed.

## The Predator in the Woods

By day, Johannes looked like a man.

At night, he attacked travelers.

Under the full moon, he hunted them.

Survivors described a huge wolf capable of standing on two legs.

They spoke of yellow eyes, long arms, and a mouth filled with strangely human teeth.

The creature did not simply kill.

It played with its victims.

First, it followed them between the trees.

Branches cracked, but nothing could be seen.

Then it appeared for a moment beside the path.

If the traveler walked faster, the creature accelerated.

If the traveler stopped, every sound vanished.

Until the animal's breath came from directly behind.

The residents locked their doors after sunset.

Livestock was brought inside.

Children were forbidden to leave their homes at night.

Then a farmer found several dead sheep.

The bodies had not been eaten.

They were arranged in a circle.

At the center was a human footprint.

The villagers realized they were not dealing with an ordinary wolf.

## The Hunt

A group of local men decided to track Johannes.

They carried axes, rifles, and torches.

A priest gave them holy water and a consecrated silver bullet.

During a full moon, they followed the creature's trail into the forest.

First, they found blood.

Then they heard a howl so loud that several men wanted to flee.

The group continued.

At a crossroads, Johannes appeared between the trees.

He was larger than any wolf they had seen.

The creature rose and leaped.

Several shots were fired.

Ordinary bullets barely slowed it.

Johannes dragged one hunter to the ground and vanished into the forest.

The men pursued him to a clearing.

There, the consecrated bullet struck.

The creature collapsed.

When the villagers approached, no wolf lay before them.

Only Johannes.

Naked.

Wounded.

Human again.

Before he died, he cursed them.

“I will return.”

“Not today. Not tomorrow.”

“But when the light goes out, Morbach will belong to me again.”

## The Eternal Flame

At the place where Johannes died, the villagers built a small roadside shrine.

Inside stood an image of the Virgin Mary.

Before it, they placed a candle.

A priest declared that the blessed flame would contain the curse.

As long as it burned, Johannes could not leave his grave.

The people made sure it never went out.

If wind or rain extinguished it, somebody relit it immediately.

As the years passed, the story became folklore.

Roads improved.

Electric light pushed back the darkness.

Few people still believed that a candle protected the region from a monster.

Then came the night of 1988.

## The Military Installation

During the Cold War, the United States operated several military facilities in the region, including installations connected to Hahn Air Base.

Sensitive equipment and military material were stored behind fences, sensors, and armed patrols.

One summer night in 1988, several American soldiers traveled to their duty post.

The moon was full.

They knew the route well.

On the way, they passed the small shrine.

One soldier had heard the local story.

Normally, a faint flame could be seen behind the glass.

That night, the shrine was dark.

The candle had gone out.

The men joked about it.

One said the werewolf was finally free.

Minutes later, the security alarm sounded.

## Something at the Fence

The system reported movement near the perimeter.

The guards assumed an animal had touched the fence.

That happened occasionally.

But the dogs reacted differently.

They did not bark.

They lowered their tails, flattened themselves against the ground, and refused to continue.

One dog began to whimper.

The soldiers approached with flashlights and weapons drawn.

At first, they saw only the fence.

Then something moved beyond it.

A large figure stood between the trees.

It was almost two meters tall.

Its legs bent unnaturally. Its arms were too long.

Dark fur covered the body.

But it stood upright like a man.

It placed both hands against the metal fence.

The fingers ended in claws.

Then it began to climb.

One soldier shouted:

“Stop!”

The creature paused.

Slowly, it turned its head.

The flashlights illuminated its face.

It had the muzzle of a wolf.

But the eyes looked human.

The creature opened its mouth and exposed its teeth.

The dogs panicked.

One nearly tore free of its handler to escape.

The soldiers raised their rifles.

Before anyone fired, the creature dropped from the fence.

It landed on all fours and disappeared into the forest.

## On the Wrong Side

The men searched outside the perimeter.

They found deep prints in the wet earth.

Some resembled wolf paws.

Others looked like bare human feet.

The trail ended at the forest.

No bushes were broken.

No tracks continued.

It was as though the creature had dissolved.

The soldiers were about to return when one heard a metallic sound behind them.

Something moved inside the secured compound.

They turned.

The figure now stood on the inner side of the fence.

Nobody had seen it cross.

The sensors had not triggered again.

The creature stood between storage buildings.

Moonlight reflected from its fur.

It raised its head and tested the air.

Then it turned toward the soldiers.

## The Attack

One man ordered it to stop.

The creature took one step.

Then another.

The soldiers moved backward.

It accelerated.

First walking.

Then running.

Finally, it charged directly at them.

The men released their safety catches.

One placed his finger on the trigger.

The creature was only meters away.

They heard its breathing.

They saw saliva between its teeth.

Around its neck hung a rusted metal tag.

Then a scream came from the forest.

A woman's scream.

High, sharp, and sudden.

The creature stopped.

Its head snapped toward the trees.

For one moment, it did not look angry.

It looked afraid.

A second scream echoed.

The creature stepped backward.

Then it disappeared between two buildings.

The soldiers followed.

There was no escape route.

Only a solid concrete wall.

The monster was gone.

## The Soldiers' Silence

The men did not know how to explain what they had seen.

A two-meter wolf walking on two legs would lead to questions, investigations, and ridicule.

They agreed on a simpler report.

A large animal had triggered the alarm and escaped into the woods.

Nothing more.

At least one soldier later spoke to local residents.

When he described the figure, they told him about the werewolf.

And the candle.

The following morning, somebody visited the shrine.

The candle had indeed gone out.

It had not burned down completely.

The wick looked as if somebody had deliberately smothered it.

Deep scratches crossed the image of the Virgin Mary.

A new candle was lit that day.

No further sightings were reported at the installation.

## What the Soldiers Did Not Report

Years later, one of the men supposedly revealed another detail.

After the new candle was lit, the soldiers found a rusted metal tag near the fence.

It resembled the object hanging around the creature's neck.

One side carried a barely readable name:

Johannes.

The other side contained no military number.

Only a sentence:

**“When the light goes out, I return.”**

The tag was handed to a superior.

Soon afterward, it disappeared.

So did the records of the alarm.

Only the men's memories remained.

And the question of what had tried to cross the fence.

## The Woman in the Forest

The scream was never explained.

The soldiers searched the forest.

They found no woman.

No footprints.

No evidence that another person had been there.

Some residents believe the voice belonged to the woman who cursed Johannes centuries earlier.

Others believe it was the Virgin Mary.

There is another possibility.

Perhaps the creature was not frightened by the voice.

Perhaps it was being called.

Perhaps the soldiers were not saved.

Perhaps the werewolf simply found another victim.

In the weeks after the incident, several drivers reported seeing a woman at the edge of the road.

She wore an old dress and stood completely still.

Anyone who stopped saw her vanish into the forest.

Soon afterward, a wolf howled in the distance.

## The Candle of Morbach

The legend of the Werewolf of Morbach is still told.

The small flame remains the symbol of the story.

Most people treat it as folklore.

Perhaps Johannes was only a criminal.

Perhaps the villagers turned him into a monster after his death.

Perhaps the soldiers saw an unusually large animal.

But that explanation leaves questions.

Why were trained guard dogs terrified?

How did the figure reach the inside of the fence?

Why did the sighting happen on the exact night the candle went out?

People continue to keep the flame burning.

Not necessarily because they believe in werewolves.

But because nobody wants to discover what happens if the story is true.

According to one later tale, the candle went out one more time during a severe storm.

A fallen tree blocked the road to the shrine.

That night, a driver reported a large figure standing in the road.

It stood on two legs.

The man braked.

The creature turned toward him.

Then it placed one hand on the hood.

The vehicle was found abandoned the next morning.

Five deep scratches cut through the metal.

The driver was gone.

Only his shoes remained, placed neatly beside the shrine.

When residents opened the door, the candle was burning again.

Nobody knew who had relit it.

Beside the wick, a claw print remained in the soft wax.

And since that night, people in the forests around Morbach occasionally hear a deep howl.

Usually during a full moon.

Usually when the flame inside the shrine begins to weaken.

Perhaps the candle is only an old tradition.

Perhaps it holds nothing back.

But if it ever goes out completely, there is only one way to learn whether the legend is true.

Wait in the darkness.

Listen for something moving between the trees.

Something that begins on four legs.

Then rises.

And remembers that Morbach was once promised to it.
