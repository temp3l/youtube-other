# Narration Script


Laura Hayes encountered viewer comments describing events before they happen. What seemed like an isolated event became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Laura Hayes was a paranormal livestream creator when the story began in an abandoned hospital. The first impossible detail was viewer comments describing events before they happen. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which gave the danger time to grow.
The first event seemed explainable. Laura starts a livestream from a sealed hospital wing. The exact time and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss.
The next incident made coincidence less convincing. Viewers begin posting warnings about rooms she has not reached. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction.
By then, the pattern was deliberate. The stream shows a second camera angle no one is operating. The recurring motif returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.
The danger became personal shortly afterward. A figure follows Laura only on the alternate feed. From that moment, the threat stopped hiding and began changing the environment.
The discovery changed the meaning of what came before. The viewer count rises despite the stream being unlisted. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.
There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Comments appear from accounts belonging to missing people. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.
The threat reacted immediately. Laura discovers the stream is delayed by exactly thirteen minutes. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.
For a moment, escape appeared possible. She uses the delay to avoid the figure and reach an exit. Survival left evidence that could be verified only in fragments.
The immediate danger seemed to end there. The live video cuts as Laura escapes. The official explanation covered practical facts but not the impossible detail.
One final detail was discovered later. Her channel continues broadcasting from inside the hospital with Laura still on screen. That final piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.
That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Laura Hayes. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
