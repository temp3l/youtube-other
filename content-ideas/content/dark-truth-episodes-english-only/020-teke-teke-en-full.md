# Narration Script

The sound began behind Nina after the last train passed: teke... teke... teke. Every time she
ran, it matched her speed.

Nina Carter was a documentary photographer when the story began in a quiet railway district in
northern Japan. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They
described a rapid scraping sound like hands striking pavement. That detail mattered because it
returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more
precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the
central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and
the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to
celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Nina photographs an old crossing
associated with a story about a woman killed on the tracks. The setting was described in enough
detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded.
Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without
explanation.

The next incident removed some of that comfort. A station worker warns her to leave before the
final freight train. A second witness or recording appeared to support part of the account, but
it also introduced a contradiction. That contradiction prevented the event from being dismissed
as fatigue, coincidence or a simple technical fault.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. After midnight, Nina hears scraping beneath the platform.
The protagonist tried a reasonable safety measure rather than moving deeper into danger. The
measure failed in a specific way, showing that the threat could anticipate normal human
responses.

The central rule was broken shortly afterward. A woman with only the upper half of her body
pulls herself from the darkness at impossible speed. From this point onward, each decision
carried a visible consequence. The recurring sound or object returned whenever the protagonist
moved closer to understanding the rule.

The discovery changed the meaning of everything that came before. The apparition asks where her
legs are and attacks anyone who answers incorrectly or hesitates. The discovery connected the
present incident to earlier victims or earlier versions of the same legend. It also revealed
that the familiar warning had been misunderstood.

There was no perfect solution, only a narrow opportunity. Nina throws her camera flash to
disorient the figure and runs through service alleys. The escape plan relied on observation
rather than physical strength. One small inconsistency offered the only way to tell the genuine
path from the trap.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. Every reflective surface shows the woman
closer than she is in reality. The threat reacted by using a trusted voice, memory or familiar
place. That made the final choice emotional as well as practical.

For several minutes, escape appeared possible. Nina reaches a crossing and realises the spirit
repeats the route of the original accident. The immediate danger ended without a complete
explanation. Authorities, relatives or witnesses could verify only fragments of what had
happened.

The official account ended there. She places the missing memorial plaque back beside the
tracks, giving the spirit her name and location. The last piece of evidence changed the meaning
of the apparent escape. It suggested that the event had continued after the protagonist
believed it was over.

One final detail was discovered later. The scraping stops, but Nina's photographs later show
two pale hands gripping the underside of every train she boards. No reliable account explains
what happened next. The surviving evidence supports more than one interpretation, and none of
them is comforting.

The account is still repeated because it leaves one question unresolved. Did Nina Carter defeat
the thing at the centre of the story, or did the encounter simply teach it how to remain
hidden? The final warning is therefore simple: when the same impossible detail appears twice,
do not wait for a third occurrence to prove that it is real.
