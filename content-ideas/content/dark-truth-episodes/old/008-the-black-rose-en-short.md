# Short 008 — Every Black Rose Predicted a Death

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Sophie found a black rose on the counter each morning. Every person named on its tag died before the flower opened.

Sophie Marek was a florist returning home after her grandmother's death when the story began in a family-owned flower shop in Prague. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described black petals appearing in locked rooms. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Sophie inherits a flower shop and discovers a sealed basement greenhouse. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
