# Short 009 — The Christmas Doll Opened Her Eyes

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.

## Narration Script

The doll arrived without a sender. Its card said, 'For the daughter who should have been mine.'

Adrian Cole was a widowed father raising his eight-year-old daughter when the story began in a snowbound family home on Christmas Eve. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a music-box version of a Christmas carol playing one note too slowly. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Adrian's daughter Lily receives an antique porcelain doll named Mary Gloria. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
