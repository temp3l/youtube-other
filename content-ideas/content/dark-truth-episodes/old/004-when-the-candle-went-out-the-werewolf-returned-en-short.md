# Short 004 — The Candle That Kept the Werewolf Away

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The old woman gave Nina one instruction: keep the candle burning until sunrise. When the flame died, something outside stood on two legs.

Nina Falk was a wildlife photographer sheltering from a blizzard when the story began in an isolated mountain cottage in the Carpathians. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a black candle whose flame bends toward the forest. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Nina becomes stranded near a remote village after photographing unusually large wolf tracks. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
