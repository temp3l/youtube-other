# Short 019 — The Truth Behind the Russian Sleep Experiment

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The experiment promised freedom if five prisoners stayed awake for thirty days. On day nine, they stopped asking to be released.

Dr. Anya Volkov was a physician assigned to observe five prisoners when the story began in a fictional Cold War research bunker. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a ventilation fan slowing as the subjects become more alert. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Researchers introduce an experimental stimulant gas into a sealed chamber. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
