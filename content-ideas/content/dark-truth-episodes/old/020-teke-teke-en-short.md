# Short 020 — She Heard Teke Teke Behind Her

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The sound began behind Nina after the last train passed: teke... teke... teke. Every time she ran, it matched her speed.

Nina Carter was a documentary photographer when the story began in a quiet railway district in northern Japan. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a rapid scraping sound like hands striking pavement. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Nina photographs an old crossing associated with a story about a woman killed on the tracks. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
