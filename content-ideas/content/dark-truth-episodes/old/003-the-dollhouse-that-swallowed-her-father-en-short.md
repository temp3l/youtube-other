# Short 003 — Her Father Disappeared Inside the Dollhouse

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Every room in Mara's dollhouse matched her home. Then a new miniature figure appeared inside it—wearing her father's clothes.

Mara Vale was a twelve-year-old girl whose father restores antique furniture when the story began in a decaying Victorian townhouse inherited by a young family. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described tiny footsteps moving through rooms that copy the real house. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Her father brings home an unfinished nineteenth-century dollhouse purchased from an estate sale. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
