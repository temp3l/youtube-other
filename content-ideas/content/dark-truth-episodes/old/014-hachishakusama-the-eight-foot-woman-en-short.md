# Short 014 — The Eight-Foot Woman Called His Name

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Clara first saw the woman's white hat moving above a two-metre wall. Then the voice calling from the garden sounded exactly like her grandmother.

Clara Bennett was a teenager visiting her grandparents when the story began in a rural Japanese village surrounded by summer fields. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a deep 'po... po... po...' heard above walls and hedges. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Clara notices an impossibly tall woman beyond her grandparents' garden wall. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
