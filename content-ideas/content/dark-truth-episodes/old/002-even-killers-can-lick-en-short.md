# Short 002 — The Killer Was Already Inside the House

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Elena believed the dog beneath her bed was calming her. By morning, the dog was dead in the hallway—and someone had written a message on the mirror.

Elena Ward was a university student house-sitting for her aunt when the story began in a rain- soaked suburban house on the edge of Brighton. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a wet tongue brushing the back of her hand from beneath the bed. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Her aunt leaves for a medical conference and asks Elena to care for Bramble, an old golden retriever who always sleeps beside the bed. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
