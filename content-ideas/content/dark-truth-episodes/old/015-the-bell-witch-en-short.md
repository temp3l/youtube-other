# Short 015 — The Voice That Haunted the Bell Family

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The voice began as scratching in the walls. Within months, it was speaking to visitors, quoting sermons and promising that John Bell would die.

John Bell and his family was a farming household at the centre of a famous American haunting legend when the story began in early nineteenth-century Tennessee farmland. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described scratching beneath the floor followed by a disembodied voice. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. The family reports strange animals, knocking sounds and invisible attacks. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
