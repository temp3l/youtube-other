# Short 021 — Something Was Watching From the Bedroom Window

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Paul's bedroom was on the second floor. There was no balcony, no tree and no ladder. Yet every night, something tapped on the glass.

Paul Mercer was a freelance editor caring for his injured brother when the story began in a remote farmhouse bordered by winter fields. The first impossible detail was fingernails tapping the upstairs window from outside. It did not look dangerous at first, which made it easier to ignore.

The account became frightening because each new incident followed a clear rule. The threat did not behave randomly. It responded to attention, repeated human choices and grew more specific whenever someone tried to explain it away.

The first incident seemed explainable. Paul moves into the farmhouse after his brother claims a pale figure watches him sleep. The exact time and location were later recorded, making the event harder to dismiss as a vague memory.
