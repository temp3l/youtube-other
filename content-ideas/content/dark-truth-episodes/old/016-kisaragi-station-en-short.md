# Short 016 — The Train Stopped at a Station That Doesn't Exist

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Mara's train passed her stop and continued for twenty minutes without slowing. Then it arrived at Kisaragi Station—a place missing from every map.

Mara Lewis was an exchange student travelling home when the story began in a late-night commuter train in Japan. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a station announcement spoken with no consonants. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Mara notices the carriage is empty and the train no longer stops at familiar stations. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
