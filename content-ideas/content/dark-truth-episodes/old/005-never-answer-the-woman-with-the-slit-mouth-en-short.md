# Short 005 — She Asked, “Am I Beautiful?”

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

A masked woman asked Paul whether she was beautiful. Every answer he gave changed the street around him.

Paul Reeves was an English teacher walking home after the last train when the story began in a quiet commuter district outside Tokyo. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described the metallic click of scissors opening in an empty street. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Paul hears students discussing a woman who appears near closed stations and asks strangers a question. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
