# Short 011 — The Children Asked to Come Inside

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Two children stood outside Noah's motel room in freezing rain. They did not want shelter. They wanted permission.

Noah Price was a travelling paramedic when the story began in a remote roadside motel during a winter storm. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described two soft knocks followed by a child's rehearsed request. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Noah checks into an almost empty motel after an ambulance transfer. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
