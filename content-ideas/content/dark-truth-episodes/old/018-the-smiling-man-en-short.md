# Short 018 — The Smiling Man Followed Her Home

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The man danced beneath a streetlight with a smile too wide for his face. When Elena crossed the road, he folded sideways and followed.

Elena Brooks was a nurse walking home after a late shift when the story began in empty city streets shortly before dawn. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described hard shoe taps in an irregular dancing rhythm. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Elena sees a thin man moving in exaggerated dance steps several blocks away. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
