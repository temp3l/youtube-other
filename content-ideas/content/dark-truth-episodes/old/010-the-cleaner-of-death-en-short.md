# Short 010 — She Cleaned Rooms Before People Died

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Mia was told never to clean a room marked with a black ribbon. At 3:10 a.m., the ribbon appeared on her own apartment door.

Mia Alvarez was a new overnight housekeeper when the story began in a high-rise hotel during the night shift. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a housekeeping cart that appears outside occupied rooms. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Mia takes a night job where an elderly cleaner named Mara handles rooms no one else may enter. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
