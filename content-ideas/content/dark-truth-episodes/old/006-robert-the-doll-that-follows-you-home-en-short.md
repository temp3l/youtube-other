# Short 006 — The Doll Followed Her Home

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

Laura filmed the doll without asking permission. That night, it appeared in the background of a photograph taken inside her locked apartment.

Laura Chen was a travel videographer researching allegedly haunted objects when the story began in a museum archive in Key West and a nearby rental apartment. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a soft fabric rustle followed by a camera shutter. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Laura visits a museum display and dismisses the custom of asking the doll's permission before photographing it. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
