# Short 007 — The Save File Knew His Real Name

## Narration Instructions

- Use the same narrator as the full episode.
- Keep the pace close to 180 words per minute.
- Begin immediately and keep the hook visible from the first sentence.

## Narration Script

The cartridge had one save file. It used Felix's childhood nickname—a name no one online knew.

Felix Grant was a software developer restoring an unlabelled game cartridge when the story began in a cramped apartment filled with retro game hardware. The first reports did not describe an obvious monster or violent attack. They described a reversed three-note game melody. That detail mattered because it returned at moments when no ordinary source could be found.

Most frightening stories become exaggerated after they are repeated. This one became more precise. Dates, rooms, recordings and witness statements differed in minor ways, but the central sequence remained the same. A warning was ignored, an impossible pattern appeared, and the person at the centre of it survived with evidence that made survival difficult to celebrate.

The first event seemed harmless enough to rationalise. Felix buys an unlabelled cartridge from a closing flea market stall. The setting was described in enough detail to make the account testable: the time, weather and nearby objects were all recorded. Later, those ordinary details became important because several of them changed without explanation.
