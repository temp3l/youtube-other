# Kisaragi Station — YouTube Short

Mara’s train passed her stop and continued for twenty minutes without slowing.

Then it arrived at Kisaragi Station—a place missing from every map.

The platform clock ran backward. A one-legged man appeared on the tracks, then vanished when a real train horn sounded. Online warnings told Mara to avoid the tunnel, but a message from her own account said something worse:

DO NOT TRUST THE ROAD.

A driver offered to help. His reflection did not match his movements.

Mara realised the station could copy faces and voices, but not sound. She escaped by following the loudest rail noise through the tunnel.

A train arrived filled with passengers who all looked like Mara at different ages.

She refused to board.

The next morning, Mara woke on her normal commuter train. Her messages from the night were still online—but their timestamps covered nine years.

Months later, her apartment intercom crackled.

“Next stop: Kisaragi.”
