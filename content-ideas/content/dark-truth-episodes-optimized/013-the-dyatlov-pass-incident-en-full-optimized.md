Nine experienced hikers cut their tent open from the inside and fled into deadly cold without proper clothing. The question is not whether they were frightened. It is what made the tent feel more dangerous than the storm.

In February 1959, a group led by Igor Dyatlov travelled into the northern Ural Mountains. They were experienced Soviet hikers attempting a demanding winter route. One member turned back because of illness. The remaining nine never returned.

At first, the detail seemed small enough to dismiss. Search teams found the group's tent on the slope of Kholat Syakhl. It had been cut from within.
