# Narration Script

Noah's daughter built the snowman facing away from the cabin. By morning, it was staring through the
kitchen window. No footprints surrounded it after fresh snow. Its buttons, scarf and eyes had been
taken from locked rooms. The behaviour followed one rule: The figure moved closer whenever the
family argued and kept whatever emotional heat they gave it. Frozen clothing inside the snow
belonged to someone missing from the village. The situation became personal after that. The snowman
appeared beside the bedroom window after the worst argument. Then the recurring sound returned from
a place it could not physically reach. The only workable plan was this: The family dismantled it
calmly and returned every borrowed object without speaking in anger. Each piece tried to pull itself
together while the temperature inside the cabin dropped. Noah kept everyone talking softly until the
final lump of snow melted. By dawn, only ordinary snow remained. Months later, a wet carrot appeared
outside their apartment door.
