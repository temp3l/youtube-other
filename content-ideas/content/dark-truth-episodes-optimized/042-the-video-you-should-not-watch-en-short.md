# Narration Script

The unlabelled video showed an empty room. In the final frame, Mia was sitting in it, watching the
screen. Each replay added details from Mia's apartment that had not been visible before. Previous
viewers had disappeared within twenty-four hours of watching the complete file. The pattern followed
one rule: The video recorded its viewer several hours in the future, and playback made that future
more difficult to change. Nearby screens began showing the footage even though the archive network
was physically offline. Mia responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating
people from the source and calling for help. At this point, the account accelerated. The only
workable plan was this: Mia edited the final frames so the future room remained empty and replaced
herself with a generated duplicate. The file accepted the edit, then began rendering a new sequence
showing Mia making the edit. She realised every attempt to change the ending became part of it. At
midnight, playback stopped one second before her predicted disappearance. Mia survived.
