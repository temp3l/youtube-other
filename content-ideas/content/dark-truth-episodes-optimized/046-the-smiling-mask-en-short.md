# Narration Script

The actor put on the porcelain mask for rehearsal. When the scene ended, the smile stayed on his
face. The actor could not remove the mask, and his voice changed to match recordings of earlier
performers. Audience members smiled involuntarily whenever he faced them. The pattern followed one
rule: The mask transferred during applause and erased emotions that did not match its painted
expression. Old programmes showed the same mask across decades, each production ending with an
unexplained disappearance. The actor forgot grief, fear and eventually the names of people he loved.
Clara responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source
and calling for help. At this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was this: Clara
staged an empty performance with no mirrors, no audience and soundproofing around the auditorium.
The masked actor performed to rows of empty seats. Recorded applause began beneath the stage, and
the mask turned toward Clara. The actor was freed. Clara kept one fragment for analysis.
