# Narration Script

Jonas received a voicemail timestamped twenty-four hours in the future. It was his own voice telling
him not to answer the door. The warning was already present: The message ended with one instruction:
do not try to prevent everything. A second voicemail predicted a power failure to the exact second.
Each warning prevented one event but created a worse consequence elsewhere. The behaviour followed
one rule: Every attempt to change the future created the next message. The calls originated from a
disconnected phone booth scheduled for demolition. The situation became personal after that. Jonas
began remembering two versions of the same day, both equally real. Then the recurring sound returned
from a place it could not physically reach. The only workable plan was this: He recorded a message
ordering his earlier self to ignore every warning. At midnight, the booth rang and displayed Jonas's
own number. As he spoke, both timelines overlapped and the city around him flickered between
outcomes. For several seconds, the outcome remained uncertain. By morning, the messages were gone.
