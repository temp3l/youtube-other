The portrait showed a woman facing a window. After Clara removed the first layer of grime, the woman
had turned to face the room. Under ultraviolet light, Clara found five earlier versions of the same
room. The pattern followed one rule: The figure advanced whenever someone studied her face. David
vanished during a power cut. The next morning, a second figure stood behind the painted woman,

Clara to navigate by reflections in pools of wet varnish. Clara sealed the work beneath opaque
primer and placed it in cold storage. Months later, a museum scan showed the woman standing beneath
the white layer, one hand pressed outward—and Clara's red mug now sat on the other side of the
glass.
