# Narration Script

The road sign changed its destination while Mara watched. The navigation voice calmly said,
'Continue toward Home.' The same petrol station appeared three times despite constant forward
driving. Road signs began showing places from Mara's childhood instead of towns. The pattern
followed one rule: Every turn toward a familiar destination removed distance from the odometer and
erased part of the driver's connection to the real world. The odometer counted backward toward zero
while her contacts and saved addresses disappeared. Mara responded with another practical measure:
stopping the work, separating people from the source and calling for help. The only workable plan
was this: Mara ignored road markings and navigated by the moon and the car's compass. The passenger
warned that zero would erase her from every destination. At one kilometre, the road split into signs
for her mother's house, her childhood school and an unmarked dirt track. She reached a real highway
with one kilometre remaining. Days later, the navigation app saved the impossible route under
'Home.' Each night, its remaining distance decreased by a few metres.
