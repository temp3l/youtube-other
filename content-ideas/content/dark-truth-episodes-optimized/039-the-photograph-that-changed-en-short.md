# Narration Script

The old family photograph changed every night. First a stranger appeared near the trees. A blurred
man advanced toward the family in every new print. Relatives who vanished from the image also
disappeared from albums, letters and other people's memories. The pattern followed one rule: The
photograph did not alter the past directly. The stranger resembled a brother Felix's grandmother
insisted had never existed. The final enlargement showed Felix behind the camera in 1978, although
he had not been born. Felix responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating
people from the source and calling for help. At this point, the account accelerated. The only
workable plan was this: Felix returned to the original field and recreated the camera angle without
developing another copy. At sunset, the picnic appeared around him like a double exposure. Felix
photographed the empty space where the stranger stood, then opened the camera without winding the
film. The missing relatives returned to the archive, but Felix appeared among them as a child. The
next morning, his identification, employment records and apartment lease were gone.
