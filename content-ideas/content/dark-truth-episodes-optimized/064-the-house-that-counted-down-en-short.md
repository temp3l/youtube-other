# Narration Script

A painted number appeared in the hallway and changed from twelve to eleven while Mara photographed
it. The warning was already present: A demolition report said no one should remain inside when the
count reached three. Every clock in the house matched the countdown. A room vanished whenever the
number decreased. The behaviour followed one rule: Each number represented a memory the house had
taken from someone inside. Old records showed previous owners disappearing when the count reached
zero. The situation became personal after that. The final room contained photographs of Mara living
in the house for years. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically
reach. The only workable plan was this: She photographed every remaining room and used the images to
preserve the layout. At one, the exits collapsed into the final room. Mara projected the photographs
in sequence, forcing the house to remember spaces it had erased long enough for her to escape. For
several seconds, the outcome remained uncertain. The house collapsed inward. Her camera later
contained one final image labelled zero.
