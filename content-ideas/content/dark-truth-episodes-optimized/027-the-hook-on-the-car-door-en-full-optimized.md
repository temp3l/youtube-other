The attempted solution did not erase the danger. It revealed what the danger had been waiting for. They acted quickly, but the result showed that speed alone was useless. The danger was not chasing their bodies; it was steering their decisions.

For a few minutes, the plan appeared to work. The outside version looked directly at the camera and mouthed the same warning heard on the phone. The temporary silence created the most dangerous moment, because it encouraged the survivors to believe the worst had passed. For a moment, ordinary background noise returned and the space looked normal again. That false calm allowed the next change to arrive without warning.

The survivor was left with proof strong enough to remember and incomplete enough to doubt.

The final piece of evidence arrived later. That night, Noah found it hanging from his bedroom door. The hook was on the inside.
