# Narration Script

The iron door had no hinges, no frame and no room behind it on any plan. One night, its handle
moved. The metal grew warm whenever anyone lied nearby. Voices behind it repeated questions Emma had
not yet asked. The pattern followed one rule: The door created a matching corridor whenever
disbelief weakened. Portraits in the museum gradually changed to show Emma as a curator from earlier
centuries. Emma heard her own future voice begging her not to turn the handle. Emma responded with
another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and calling for
help. At this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was this: She documented the
wall depth, sealed surrounding rooms and prepared a rope line before testing the lock. The lock
opened when Emma said, 'There is nothing there.' Beyond it lay the same corridor, older and darker,
with another Emma running toward her. The two women reached the threshold simultaneously. The museum
bricked over the wall. Days later, a sealed iron door appeared inside Emma's apartment.
