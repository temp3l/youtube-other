# Narration Script

Elena raised her hand. Her reflection lowered its own and pointed behind her. During a video call,
Elena's reflection smiled before Elena did. It began performing actions Elena had not decided to
make, then waited for her to copy them. The pattern followed one rule: Every action Elena repeated
after the reflection made it transferred a small part of her life to the other side. Dark laptop
screens showed a second apartment where another Elena lived with altered photographs and reversed
writing. Elena found childhood memories missing after copying the reflection's gestures. Her mother
remembered a hospital stay Elena could not recall. At this point, the account accelerated. The only
workable plan was this: She covered every reflective surface and tested the other Elena with private
scars, habits and memories. The reflection displayed the same scar on the wrong side, then cut its
palm. A matching wound opened on Elena's hand. The glass fell outward, not inward. Elena woke alone
among the fragments.
