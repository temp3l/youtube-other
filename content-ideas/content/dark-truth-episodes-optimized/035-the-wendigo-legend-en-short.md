# Narration Script

Jonas expected a story about a creature in the woods. The historian told him the real warning was
about what hunger could turn a person into. The pattern followed one rule: The presence grew
stronger whenever someone hoarded food, abandoned another person or treated suffering as
entertainment. They found the trapper alive, emaciated and refusing food because he believed feeding
himself would feed something else. Jonas responded with another practical measure: stopping the
work, separating people from the source and calling for help. The only workable plan was this:
Rather than hunting a creature, Mara organised a retreat based on marked trees, shared supplies and
keeping every person in sight. A blizzard separated Jonas. His own voice called from a warm-looking
cabin and promised safety if he kept the last food pack. Jonas survived and released a documentary
centred on cultural context, greed and isolation rather than monster spectacle. Months later, during
a business meeting, he heard the same voice from an office corridor whenever someone said there was
not enough to share.
