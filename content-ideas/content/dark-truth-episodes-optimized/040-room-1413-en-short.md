# Narration Script

The hotel had no fourteenth floor, but Sophie's keycard was printed with Room 1413. The lift opened
onto a narrow corridor where the wallpaper and lighting belonged to another decade. Room 1413
contained luggage from guests reported missing over nearly a century. The pattern followed one rule:
The room trapped people at the moment they decided to abandon their current lives. The telephone
rang repeatedly, and voices of former guests begged her to confirm checkout for them. Sophie
responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and
calling for help. The only workable plan was this: Sophie used the room phone to call the present-
day lobby and kept the line open as an anchor. The receptionist guided her toward a service door
that existed only while both women described the same corridor. Past versions of the hotel flickered
around Sophie, and the caller's voice aged and grew younger between sentences. Sophie escaped before
the connection failed. Her review of the hotel later appeared online with photographs no one
remembered her taking.
