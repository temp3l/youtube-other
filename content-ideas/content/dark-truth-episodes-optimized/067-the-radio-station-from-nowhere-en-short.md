# Narration Script

A second station began broadcasting on Jonas's frequency. It announced a car crash before the
emergency call arrived. The warning was already present: An archived memo ordered staff never to
repeat the dead station's warnings on air. The voice used a call sign retired decades earlier. Each
prediction became more personal. The behaviour followed one rule: Every warning repeated by a living
host became inevitable. Archived tapes contained the same voice before earlier disasters. The
situation became personal after that. The final broadcast predicted Jonas would repeat every
message. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically reach. The only
workable plan was this: He shut down his own station and refused to relay the last warning. The old
transmitter beneath the building continued without power. Jonas destroyed the feed while his own
voice began speaking from the dead frequency. For several seconds, the outcome remained uncertain.
The station went silent. The next night, Jonas's voice returned on the retired call sign. The story
remains disturbing because the final evidence did not show a clean escape.
