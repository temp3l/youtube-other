# Narration Script

Daniel burned the box, buried the ashes and left town. The next morning, it was waiting on his
kitchen table. Daniel threw it into the lake. He mailed it overseas, but the box returned before the
tracking page showed the parcel leaving the country. The pattern followed one rule: Every failed
attempt to abandon it removed one object, memory or relationship from Daniel's life. After the third
return, photographs no longer showed his childhood dog. The knocking inside the box matched a
sequence in his grandfather's notes. It spelled Daniel's name, then the word OPEN. At this point,
the account accelerated. The only workable plan was this: Daniel used the pattern to locate hidden
pressure points and opened the box without breaking it. Inside was a perfect miniature of the cabin.
A smaller Daniel stood at the table holding another box. Daniel set fire to the cabin while holding
the open box over the flames. Firefighters found no body and no foundation—only the undamaged box in
a circle of wet earth.
