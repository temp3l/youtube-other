# Narration Script

The emergency broadcast cut in without authorisation. Instead of a weather warning, it displayed
Mara's name and the time 4:12 a.m. The signal listed local addresses seconds before power failed at
each one. The pattern followed one rule: Anything broadcast before 4:12 became true. The other staff
evacuated, yet studio cameras continued following invisible movement through deserted rooms. Mara
responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and
calling for help. The only workable plan was this: Mara routed the signal into a closed internal
loop and replaced the live output with prerecorded weather footage. The presenter changed scripts
and began narrating Mara's movements in real time. When Mara reached for the master switch, the
screen showed her doing it first—and showed the ceiling collapsing. At exactly 4:12, every monitor
went black and the storm stopped. Mara survived, but one frame in every programme aired by the
station now shows her sitting alone in the control room, staring at something behind the camera.
