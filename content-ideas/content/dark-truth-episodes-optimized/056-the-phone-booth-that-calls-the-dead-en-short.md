# Narration Script

The phone rang only during storms. When Sophie answered, she heard her dead brother say her name.
The warning was already present: Names carved into the booth warned callers never to ask the same
question twice. The voice knew details never published about her brother's death. Every call cost
Sophie one memory of him. The behaviour followed one rule: The booth traded memories for answers and
allowed other voices to impersonate the dead. Previous callers had carved names and dates into the
metal frame. The situation became personal after that. Sophie began forgetting her brother's face
while learning how he died. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically
reach. The only workable plan was this: She asked a question the real brother would refuse to
answer. The false voice became furious and every telephone line in the booth began ringing at once.
Sophie held the receiver away and waited for the one line that remained silent. She hung up before
losing her final memory. During the next storm, the booth called from Sophie's own number.
