# Narration Script

The mirror in Room Six did not show the present. It showed the room one day ahead. The mirror showed
the room empty while Tom stood in front of it. It later revealed damage that had not happened yet.
The behaviour followed one rule: Every attempt to prevent the reflected future created a different
path to the same event. A guest appeared in the mirror twenty-four hours before checking in. The
situation became personal after that. Tom saw himself unconscious on the floor at the exact time the
next day. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically reach. The only
workable plan was this: He removed the mirror and prevented anyone from entering Room Six. The
reflected room continued existing in dark windows throughout the hotel. Tom followed those
reflections to the service corridor and interrupted the cause rather than the predicted result. The
accident did not happen, but Room Six disappeared from the hallway. Tom still saw it reflected in
every dark window.
