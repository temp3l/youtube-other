# Narration Script

Tom never saw the tall man with his own eyes. He appeared only in photographs, and in every new
image he was closer. The figure first appeared behind volunteers searching for a missing teenager.
Tom found the same shape in newspaper negatives from 1968, 1987 and 2004, always beside someone who
vanished within days. The pattern followed one rule: The man could move only between captured
images. His camera started displaying photographs several seconds before he took them. One preview
showed the man standing inside Tom's locked house. At this point, the account accelerated. The only
workable plan was this: Tom printed every affected frame and arranged them in reverse order, hoping
to force the figure backward through its own path. The radio tower began transmitting shutter sounds
while the prints changed on the wall. Tom burned the newest photograph first. Tom published the
entire archive at once, believing millions of viewers would scatter the entity. By morning, every
human face in the photographs had vanished except one.
