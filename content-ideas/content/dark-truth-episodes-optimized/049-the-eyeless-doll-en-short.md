# Narration Script

The doll had no eyes, yet every time Daniel turned away, its empty sockets were facing him. Each
socket held miniature photographs of previous owners instead of glass eyes. People near the doll
began losing details from their vision: colours, faces and finally depth. The pattern followed one
rule: The doll saw through anyone who looked directly into it and stored each stolen viewpoint as a
photograph. Daniel's assistant could no longer recognise faces, while the doll's carved features
became more lifelike. Daniel responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating
people from the source and calling for help. The only workable plan was this: Daniel sealed the
sockets with wax and placed the doll in a mirrored box so it could see only itself. The mirrors
filled with hundreds of photographed eyes. The doll began turning inside the sealed case, and
Daniel's vision narrowed to two circular tunnels. The missing vision returned and the doll became
still. A week later, Daniel found his own photograph inside a socket that remained sealed with
unbroken wax.
