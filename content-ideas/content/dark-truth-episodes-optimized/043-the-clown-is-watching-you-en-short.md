# Narration Script

Noah saw the clown inside a locked toy shop. When he checked the next camera, it was already waiting
in another corridor. The clown's painted smile widened whenever Noah looked away. It copied his
patrol route several minutes before he walked it. The pattern followed one rule: The figure
travelled through reflections, not hallways. Emergency exits opened back into the central atrium,
where the clown appeared in dozens of black windows. A former guard's radio voice guided Noah toward
safety, but security records showed that guard had died in 1998. Noah responded with another
practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and calling for help. At
this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was this: Noah shut down the atrium
lighting and covered reflective surfaces using maintenance tarps. The carnival music slowed as the
final window disappeared. The clown emerged in the polished floor beneath Noah's feet. The centre
reopened months later. Noah no longer worked there, but his reflection in photographs wore white
face paint.
