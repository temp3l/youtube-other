# Narration Script

The aerial photograph showed a complete village. When Lukas reached the valley, every meal was still
warm—but every person was gone. Every clock in the village had stopped at 2:15 a.m. Family
photographs contained blank spaces shaped like missing people. The pattern followed one rule: The
village disappeared whenever outsiders remembered and documented it too completely. Lukas's
recordings began deleting themselves from the oldest file forward. Lukas responded with another
practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and calling for help. At
this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was this: He wrote names and house
numbers across his skin, using memory distributed across fragments instead of one complete record.
At 2:15, the bell rang and the buildings became transparent. Lukas drove out while reciting only
every second name, leaving the child to complete the sequence from inside the valley. Authorities
found an empty field. Lukas could still remember the village, but he no longer remembered his own
hometown.
