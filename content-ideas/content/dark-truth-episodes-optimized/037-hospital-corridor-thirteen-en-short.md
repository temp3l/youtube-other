# Narration Script

The lift opened onto Corridor Thirteen. The hospital had only twelve floors. Paul followed the alert
and found occupied rooms with curtains closed but no visible patients. Charts displayed admission
dates from the future and diagnoses written in Paul's own handwriting. The pattern followed one
rule: Once a monitor matched a visitor's pulse, the corridor began rewriting that person as a
patient. An empty bed displayed Paul's heart rhythm before he touched any sensor. Staff on the
corridor insisted Paul had been unconscious there for weeks. Security footage showed him arriving on
a trolley the previous night. At this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was
this: Paul disconnected the central telemetry bridge so the ward could no longer maintain a
consistent identity for him. Every room door opened. Inside each was Paul at a different age,
suffering a different possible death. He returned to Floor Twelve before 3:18. His employee badge
still worked, but his personnel file had been replaced by a discharge summary.
