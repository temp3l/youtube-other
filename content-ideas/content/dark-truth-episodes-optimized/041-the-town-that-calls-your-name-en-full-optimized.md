# Narration Script

The first voice called Adrian's name from an empty house. The second came from behind him.

Adrian Vale was a travel writer when the account began in an abandoned mountain town hidden after
sunset. The object at the centre of it was a town where every place card bore Adrian's name. It did
not arrive with violence, a body or a clear threat. It entered the story through routine work, which
gave Adrian every reason to remain calm. Later, that ordinary beginning became important because the
earliest details were the only ones that never changed.

The warning was already there: An elderly resident told him never to answer any voice that called
from behind. Adrian did what most careful people would do. The instruction was photographed, logged
and treated as a precaution left by someone superstitious. Nothing in the room appeared damaged. No
alarm had triggered. The doors, windows and locks were all in the condition described by the
records.

The road disappeared from his map as soon as the sun went down. Adrian repeated the test rather than
trusting a first impression. Equipment was restarted, measurements were taken again and timestamps
were compared. The result remained the same. That repetition was more disturbing than a sudden shock
because it removed the easiest explanation: simple error.

For a while, the incident could still be contained. Adrian stepped away, returned later and asked
another person to observe without being told what to expect. That observer noticed a different
detail, but it pointed in the same direction. The account was no longer based on one frightened
person's memory.

A public loudspeaker announced private memories only Adrian knew. The episode's recurring sounds—a
distant town bell, house timbers creaking, and a voice crossing an empty street—began appearing only
at the moment the pattern advanced. They stayed low in the background, never announcing exactly what
had changed. Their purpose was to make the audience recognise danger before the characters did.

The pattern followed one rule: Whenever Adrian spoke his own name, streets rearranged and public
records outside the town forgot another detail about him. Once Adrian understood that rule, the
earlier incidents stopped looking random. They formed a sequence: observation, response, escalation
and correction. The threat was testing how a reasonable person reacted and changing its behaviour
after every failed attempt to control it.

Adrian began an investigation rather than waiting for another incident. Old records, archived
messages, previous owners and maintenance reports were compared. The dates were separated by years,
sometimes decades, but the language was almost identical. Different witnesses had described the same
timing, the same repeated sound and the same moment when a harmless anomaly became personal.

The records also revealed what earlier victims had done wrong. Some tried to destroy the object
immediately. Others shared it, moved away or convinced themselves the pattern had ended. None of
those actions broke the rule. They only changed where the next incident occurred.

Adrian tried the safest option first. The source was isolated, the location was secured and an
outside contact was told exactly where to look if communication stopped. For several minutes, the
measure appeared successful. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically
reach.

A registry listed previous visitors whose names had been erased line by line. This was the first
piece of evidence that survived outside the protagonist's perception. It could be photographed,
copied or compared. It did not prove a supernatural explanation, but it proved the official
explanation was incomplete.

The discovery also changed the emotional stakes. Until then, Adrian had been protecting a job, a
reputation or a piece of evidence. Now the threat had reached into personal history and selected
details designed to produce hesitation. Leaving was still possible, but leaving someone or something
behind was no longer simple.

His phone contacts lost his surname, then his photographs, then every message he had sent. Adrian
responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the source and
calling for help. The response failed in a specific way. The failure revealed that distance alone
would not end the pattern.

At this point, the account accelerated. Familiar exits became unreliable. Ordinary background noise
disappeared between incidents. Small details shifted position whenever nobody was watching. The
location seemed less like a haunted place and more like a mechanism closing around one person.

The only workable plan was this: Adrian recorded a false name and played it through the town
loudspeaker. It relied on observation instead of physical strength. The goal was not to destroy
something that did not obey ordinary limits. The goal was to force it into repeating the one
behaviour it could not change.

Before acting, Adrian tested the plan on a smaller scale. The result created a brief opening and
confirmed the rule, but it also warned the threat. From that moment forward, every sound and
movement arrived faster. The entity no longer waited to be noticed.

Every house began calling the false name while he ran toward the boundary. Then his dead father's
voice called his real name from behind. Adrian nearly answered, but rang the town bell instead,
drowning the voice long enough to cross the road before sunrise. Adrian had only one opportunity to
commit to the plan. Reversing course would have repeated the earlier mistake and strengthened the
trap. The final action worked not because it was powerful, but because it contradicted what the
threat expected a frightened person to do.

For several seconds, the location became completely silent. The recurring motif stopped. Lights,
clocks or electronic displays returned to normal. Adrian did not interpret that silence as victory.
It felt more like the moment after a machine had finished recording.

The road returned and Adrian's records slowly reappeared. Weeks later, strangers in his city began
turning toward him before anyone spoke. On quiet nights, empty buildings called the false name he
had left behind.

In the hours after the escape, Adrian documented everything while the sequence was still fresh. The
notes were duplicated, timestamps were preserved and physical evidence was stored separately. By
morning, several details had already changed. A name was missing from one copy. A time differed by
one minute. A photograph showed an object in the wrong position.

Investigators, relatives or employers later verified fragments of the account. They could confirm
times, damaged equipment, missing records or physical traces, but not the sequence that connected
them. The official report reduced the event to stress, malfunction, trespassing or an unexplained
absence.

The surviving version of the town that calls your name after dark continues to be repeated because
it leaves one practical warning. When an impossible detail repeats under controlled conditions, it
is no longer harmless simply because it has not attacked yet. The most disturbing possibility is not
that the danger survived Adrian's escape. It is that the escape taught it a quieter way to follow
the next person.
