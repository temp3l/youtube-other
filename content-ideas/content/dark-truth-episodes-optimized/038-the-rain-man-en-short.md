Every photograph of the flood showed the same man. He stood dry beneath the rain, always facing
Laura's house. Witnesses described different faces but the same coat and the same impossible
dryness. Laura found him in childhood photographs taken during the storm that killed her father. The
pattern followed one rule: The man came to collect a life promised during an earlier rescue. Her
mother admitted that when Laura nearly drowned as a child, the family had invited the man inside and
offered 'someone else' in exchange. Laura responded with another practical measure: stopping the
work, separating people from the source and calling for help. At this point, the account

live, believing public knowledge would break the private contract. The storm stopped outside, but
rain began falling inside the television studio. The man stepped through the live weather screen,
dry as ever. The broadcast ended in static. Laura vanished, but no one else disappeared that night.
