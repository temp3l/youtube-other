# Narration Script

Everyone remembered that the story terrified them. Nobody could remember what happened in it. The
handwriting changed between paragraphs, as though different readers had continued the same account.
People named in the text disappeared from photographs, databases and memories. The pattern followed
one rule: The story preserved itself by replacing real people with characters, then erasing anyone
who could describe the plot. The final page predicted the order in which Tom's friends would forget
him. Tom responded with another practical measure: stopping the work, separating people from the
source and calling for help. At this point, the account accelerated. The only workable plan was
this: He removed his name from every copy and replaced it with a fictional character. The catalogue
immediately listed the fictional character as the author. Tom's colleagues began discussing him as
someone from a book. Tom survived, but friends remembered him only as a vague character. The
manuscript later gained a new first line: 'Tom Avery first heard about this from you.' Beneath it
was the viewer's current date.
