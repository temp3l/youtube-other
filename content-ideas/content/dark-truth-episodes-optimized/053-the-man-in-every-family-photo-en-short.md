# Narration Script

The same unknown man appeared in photographs taken across five generations. His clothes changed.
Relatives remembered the stranger but could not agree on his name. Each restored image placed him
closer to the photographer. The behaviour followed one rule: He appeared whenever one family member
was forgotten. A damaged birth record listed him as witness to every birth in the family. The
situation became personal after that. When Paul restored missing names, his own began fading from
current records. Then the recurring sound returned from a place it could not physically reach. The
only workable plan was this: He rebuilt the complete family tree and returned every erased person to
the archive. The stranger disappeared from the oldest images first, moving forward through time
until the final photograph changed while Paul watched. Paul had one opportunity to commit to the
plan. The last image showed Paul standing where the stranger had been. Future family photographs
contained Paul long after his recorded death.
