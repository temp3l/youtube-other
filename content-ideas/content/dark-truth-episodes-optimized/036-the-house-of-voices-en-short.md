# Narration Script

The house did not echo Nina's words. It repeated them several seconds before she said them. Basement
tapes contained conversations dated decades before the manor was built. Rooms began replaying voices
of people who had died there, including details no surviving witness knew. The pattern followed one
rule: The house learned a speaker by hearing their voice, then predicted their next words until
prediction became control. Microphones were hidden throughout the walls, but none had cables,
capsules or power. The locked booth repeated Nina's private thoughts in her own voice. Soon she
could not tell whether she was thinking a sentence or hearing it first. At this point, the account
accelerated. The only workable plan was this: Nina recorded one minute of deliberate silence, hoping
to give the house nothing to imitate. The silent file contained a future emergency call reporting
Nina missing. She overloaded the archive with feedback while refusing to speak. The studio was
condemned. Weeks later, Nina received a blank audio attachment.
