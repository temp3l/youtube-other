# Narration Script

Clara Mills encountered a porcelain mask with a fixed smile. What seemed like an isolated incident became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Clara Mills was a theatre costume designer when the story began in an old municipal theatre. The first impossible detail was a porcelain mask with a fixed smile. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which was exactly why the danger had time to grow.

The first incident appeared explainable. Clara finds the mask inside a locked wardrobe beneath the stage. The time, place and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss as a vague memory.

The next event made coincidence less convincing. An actor wears it during rehearsal and cannot remove it afterward. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction no ordinary explanation could resolve.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. The mask changes the actor's voice and makes every audience member smile. The recurring sound or object returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.

The warning was broken shortly afterward. Old programmes show the same mask appearing in productions decades apart. From that moment, the danger stopped waiting to be noticed and began changing the environment.

The discovery changed the meaning of everything that came before. The wearer begins forgetting emotions that do not match the painted expression. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.

There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Clara learns the mask survives by transferring itself during applause. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. She stages an empty performance with no audience and no mirrors. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.

For a moment, escape appeared possible. The mask cracks when nobody responds to its final bow. Survival left behind evidence that could be verified only in fragments.

The immediate danger seemed to end there. The actor is freed, but Clara keeps one porcelain fragment. The official explanation covered the practical facts but not the impossible detail.

One final detail was discovered later. Every photograph of Clara now shows her smiling, even when she is asleep. That last piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.

That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Clara Mills. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
