# Narration Script


Lukas Reed encountered objects losing their shadows after sunset. What seemed like an isolated event became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Lukas Reed was a real-estate assessor when the story began in an isolated manor. The first impossible detail was objects losing their shadows after sunset. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which gave the danger time to grow.
The first event seemed explainable. Lukas notices the furniture casts no shadows. The exact time and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss.
The next incident made coincidence less convincing. His own shadow disappears when he enters the dining room. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction.
By then, the pattern was deliberate. Photographs show dark figures standing where shadows should be. The recurring motif returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.
The danger became personal shortly afterward. The house grows colder each time another shadow vanishes. From that moment, the threat stopped hiding and began changing the environment.
The discovery changed the meaning of what came before. Old records say every owner left without being seen again. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.
There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Lukas finds a basement filled with moving shadows. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.
The threat reacted immediately. They imitate the actions of missing residents. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.
For a moment, escape appeared possible. He uses floodlights to force the shadows back toward their owners. Survival left evidence that could be verified only in fragments.
The immediate danger seemed to end there. His own shadow returns just before dawn. The official explanation covered practical facts but not the impossible detail.
One final detail was discovered later. Later, it begins moving a second before Lukas does. That final piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.
That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Lukas Reed. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
