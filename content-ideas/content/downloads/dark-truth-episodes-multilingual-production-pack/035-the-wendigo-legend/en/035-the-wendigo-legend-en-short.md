## Narration Script

Jonas expected a story about a creature in the woods. The historian told him the real warning
was about what hunger can turn a person into. The episode introduces the danger of reducing
diverse Algonquian traditions to a single antlered movie monster. Food stores are untouched, but
the trapper's journal describes endless hunger. Mara explains that the legend can represent
greed, isolation, taboo and consuming more than one needs. He survives by following marked trees
and sharing his remaining food with another lost hiker. The ending frames the horror as the
possibility that the hunger follows people home through greed rather than claws.
