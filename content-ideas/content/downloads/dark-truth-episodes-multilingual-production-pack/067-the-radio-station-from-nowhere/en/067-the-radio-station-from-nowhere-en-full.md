# Narration Script


Jonas Reed encountered an unknown frequency predicting local events. What seemed like an isolated event became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Jonas Reed was a late-night radio host when the story began in a rural broadcast station. The first impossible detail was an unknown frequency predicting local events. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which gave the danger time to grow.
The first event seemed explainable. Jonas hears a second station broadcasting on his own frequency. The exact time and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss.
The next incident made coincidence less convincing. The voice announces a car crash before emergency calls arrive. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction.
By then, the pattern was deliberate. Each prediction becomes more personal. The recurring motif returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.
The danger became personal shortly afterward. The station identifies itself using a call sign retired decades earlier. From that moment, the threat stopped hiding and began changing the environment.
The discovery changed the meaning of what came before. Archived tapes reveal the same voice broadcasting before previous disasters. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.
There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Jonas discovers the signal originates from beneath the station. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.
The threat reacted immediately. He finds an old transmitter running without power. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.
For a moment, escape appeared possible. The final broadcast predicts Jonas will repeat every warning. Survival left evidence that could be verified only in fragments.
The immediate danger seemed to end there. He shuts down his own station to break the cycle. The official explanation covered practical facts but not the impossible detail.
One final detail was discovered later. The next night, his voice broadcasts from the dead frequency. That final piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.
That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Jonas Reed. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
