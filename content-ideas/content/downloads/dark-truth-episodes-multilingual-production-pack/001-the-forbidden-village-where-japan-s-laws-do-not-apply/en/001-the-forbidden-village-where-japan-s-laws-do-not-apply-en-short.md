# Narration Script

At the end of an abandoned road in Japan, Daniel found a sign that said:

“Beyond this point, the laws of Japan do not apply.”

He crossed it and entered a village missing from every map.

The houses were empty, but every door had shoes outside.

Then the village loudspeaker switched on.

Daniel’s own voice came through it.

“Do not look back.”

He ran toward the road, but footsteps followed him through the darkness. Each step sounded half a second before his own foot touched the ground.

At sunrise, Daniel reached the warning sign.

Someone was waiting on the other side.

It was another Daniel.

The stranger held up a phone showing Daniel entering the village the previous day.

“You are the copy,” he said.

Behind Daniel, dozens of identical voices whispered his name.

Only one of them crossed the boundary.

Weeks later, Daniel seemed normal.

But mirrors stopped copying his movements.

And every night, his reflection whispered the same sentence:

“You let the wrong one leave.”
