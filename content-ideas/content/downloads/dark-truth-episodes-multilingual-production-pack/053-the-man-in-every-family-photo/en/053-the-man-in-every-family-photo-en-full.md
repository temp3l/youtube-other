# Narration Script

Paul Grant encountered the same unknown man appearing across five generations. What seemed like an isolated incident became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Paul Grant was a genealogist when the story began in a family archive. The first impossible detail was the same unknown man appearing across five generations. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which was exactly why the danger had time to grow.

The first incident appeared explainable. Paul notices the stranger in photographs taken decades apart. The time, place and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss as a vague memory.

The next event made coincidence less convincing. The man's clothing changes, but his face never ages. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction no ordinary explanation could resolve.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. Living relatives claim they remember him but cannot agree on his name. The recurring sound or object returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.

The warning was broken shortly afterward. Each restored photograph places him closer to the camera. From that moment, the danger stopped waiting to be noticed and began changing the environment.

The discovery changed the meaning of everything that came before. A damaged birth record lists him as witness to every family birth. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.

There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Paul discovers the stranger appears whenever one family member is forgotten. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. He creates a complete family tree and restores every missing name. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.

For a moment, escape appeared possible. The stranger disappears from the oldest photographs first. Survival left behind evidence that could be verified only in fragments.

The immediate danger seemed to end there. The final image shows Paul standing in the stranger's former place. The official explanation covered the practical facts but not the impossible detail.

One final detail was discovered later. Future family photos contain Paul long after his recorded death. That last piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.

That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Paul Grant. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
