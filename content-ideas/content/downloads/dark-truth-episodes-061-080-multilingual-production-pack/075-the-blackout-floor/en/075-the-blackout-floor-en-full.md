# Narration Script


Noah Finch encountered an unlisted floor appearing only during power failures. What seemed like an isolated event became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Noah Finch was an electrical engineer when the story began in a modern office tower. The first impossible detail was an unlisted floor appearing only during power failures. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which gave the danger time to grow.
The first event seemed explainable. Noah is trapped in an elevator during a city blackout. The exact time and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss.
The next incident made coincidence less convincing. The doors open onto a completely dark office floor. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction.
By then, the pattern was deliberate. Emergency lights reveal desks covered in personal belongings. The recurring motif returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.
The danger became personal shortly afterward. Every computer displays a different employee's final email. From that moment, the threat stopped hiding and began changing the environment.
The discovery changed the meaning of what came before. Noah hears people working in the darkness. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.
There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Building plans show no floor between Levels 18 and 19. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.
The threat reacted immediately. He follows the sound of a backup generator. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.
For a moment, escape appeared possible. The generator powers only one elevator button. Survival left evidence that could be verified only in fragments.
The immediate danger seemed to end there. Noah escapes when the city's electricity returns. The official explanation covered practical facts but not the impossible detail.
One final detail was discovered later. Afterward, his employee badge grants access to a floor marked BLACKOUT. That final piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.
That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Noah Finch. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
