# Narration Script

Adrian stepped through a maintenance door and entered an office floor that was larger than the
entire building.

Adrian Cole was a building inspector when the story began in a condemned office complex beneath
a shopping centre. The first impossible detail was fluorescent lights buzzing in rooms with no
visible power source. It did not look dangerous at first, which made it easier to ignore.

The account became frightening because each new incident followed a clear rule. The threat did
not behave randomly. It responded to attention, repeated human choices and grew more specific
whenever someone tried to explain it away.

The first incident seemed explainable. Adrian inspects a sealed service corridor after workers
report hearing office phones ring behind the walls. The exact time and location were later
recorded, making the event harder to dismiss as a vague memory.

The next event made coincidence less convincing. The door opens into yellow rooms with damp
carpet and repeating fluorescent panels. A witness, recording or physical mark supported part of
the account while introducing a contradiction.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. His laser measure shows the corridor growing longer each
time he looks away. The recurring sound or object returned closer than before and reacted to the
protagonist's decisions.

The central warning was broken shortly afterward. Exit signs lead back to the same room but with
furniture moved slightly. From that point onward, the danger stopped waiting to be noticed and
began shaping the environment.

The discovery changed the meaning of what came before. An office phone rings and plays a
recording of Adrian asking for help several hours in the future. Earlier events now appeared to
be preparation rather than isolated disturbances.

There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. He discovers handwritten maps made by
previous trapped people, all ending at different versions of the same red door. The plan
depended on observing one inconsistency instead of overpowering the threat.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. A distant figure follows only when the lights
stop buzzing. A familiar voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.

For a moment, escape appeared possible. Adrian keeps the lights active by triggering motion
sensors as he runs. Survival came with evidence that authorities or relatives could verify only
in fragments.

The immediate danger ended there. He reaches the red door and returns to the shopping centre
before rescue teams arrive. The official explanation covered the practical facts but not the
impossible detail.

One final detail was discovered later. The building plans later show a new office level labelled
with Adrian's full name. That last piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.

That is why the story continues to be repeated. The unsettling question is not only what
happened to Adrian Cole. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a
method the danger now uses to find the next person.


The setting itself contributed to the danger. Familiar exits no longer felt reliable, ordinary
background sounds disappeared, and small details seemed positioned to draw attention toward the
wrong place.

The protagonist documented what was happening instead of relying on memory. That record later
became important because the written or recorded version did not always match what had just been
experienced.

A rational explanation remained possible for each event in isolation. Together, however, the
incidents formed a sequence that behaved more like a test than a haunting.

The threat appeared to understand hesitation. It became most active whenever the protagonist
considered leaving, calling for help or admitting that the warning might be real.

One repeated detail provided the only stable reference point. Everything else could change, but
that sound, object or timing pattern remained consistent.

The surviving evidence did not prove a supernatural explanation. It did prove that the official
account left out something important.
