# Narration Script

Elena Ward encountered a distress signal from a ship missing for thirty years. What seemed like an isolated incident became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Elena Ward was a maritime journalist when the story began in a research vessel in the North Atlantic. The first impossible detail was a distress signal from a ship missing for thirty years. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which was exactly why the danger had time to grow.

The first incident appeared explainable. Elena joins a crew investigating the returning distress signal. The time, place and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss as a vague memory.

The next event made coincidence less convincing. They find the missing vessel drifting with lights and engines still active. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction no ordinary explanation could resolve.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. The logbook contains entries dated decades into the future. The recurring sound or object returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.

The warning was broken shortly afterward. Footsteps move through sealed lower decks. From that moment, the danger stopped waiting to be noticed and began changing the environment.

The discovery changed the meaning of everything that came before. The crew hear their own voices over the abandoned ship's radio. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.

There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. Elena learns the vessel repeats its final voyage until it collects a complete replacement crew. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. She overloads the navigation system with contradictory routes. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.

For a moment, escape appeared possible. The ghost ship begins moving in several directions at once. Survival left behind evidence that could be verified only in fragments.

The immediate danger seemed to end there. Elena escapes aboard the research vessel before the other ship vanishes. The official explanation covered the practical facts but not the impossible detail.

One final detail was discovered later. Her crew returns safely, but every passenger manifest lists them aboard the missing vessel. That last piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.

That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Elena Ward. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
