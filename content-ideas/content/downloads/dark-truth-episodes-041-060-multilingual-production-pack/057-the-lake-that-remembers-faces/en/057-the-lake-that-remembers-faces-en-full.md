# Narration Script

Adrian Moss encountered faces visible beneath perfectly still water. What seemed like an isolated incident became a pattern that knew exactly how to follow.

Adrian Moss was a documentary diver when the story began in a remote lake. The first impossible detail was faces visible beneath perfectly still water. It seemed harmless enough to dismiss, which was exactly why the danger had time to grow.

The first incident appeared explainable. Adrian films faces appearing beneath the surface at night. The time, place and physical evidence were recorded, making the account difficult to dismiss as a vague memory.

The next event made coincidence less convincing. Local families recognise people who vanished near the lake. A second witness or recording supported part of the story while creating a contradiction no ordinary explanation could resolve.

By then, the pattern was deliberate. The reflections move even when the water is disturbed. The recurring sound or object returned closer than before and reacted to the protagonist's decisions.

The warning was broken shortly afterward. Adrian sees his own face among them before entering the water. From that moment, the danger stopped waiting to be noticed and began changing the environment.

The discovery changed the meaning of everything that came before. A submerged village lies beneath the deepest point. Earlier events now looked like preparation rather than isolated disturbances.

There was no safe solution, only a narrow opportunity. The lake preserves the final memory of everyone who dies nearby. The plan depended on recognising one stable rule instead of overpowering the threat.

The threat recognised the plan almost immediately. Adrian finds a camera containing footage from his future dive. A trusted voice, memory or place was used to make the wrong choice feel safe.

For a moment, escape appeared possible. He refuses to follow the exact route shown in the footage. Survival left behind evidence that could be verified only in fragments.

The immediate danger seemed to end there. The faces guide him toward a trapped living diver instead. The official explanation covered the practical facts but not the impossible detail.

One final detail was discovered later. After the rescue, every mirror Adrian owns shows rippling water. That last piece of evidence changed the meaning of the apparent escape.

That is why the story is still repeated. The disturbing question is not only what happened to Adrian Moss. It is whether the final warning describes a danger that ended—or a method the danger now uses to find the next person.
