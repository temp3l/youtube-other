# Never Answer the Woman With the Slit Mouth

In Japan, people tell stories about a woman wearing a white mask.

She waits on empty streets at night.

Then she asks:

**“Am I beautiful?”**

Say no, and she attacks.

Say yes, and she removes the mask.

Her mouth has been cut from ear to ear.

Then she asks:

**“What about now?”**

Say yes again, and she gives you the same smile.

Say no, and you die.

In 1979, fear spread so widely that some children walked to school only in groups.

People claim you can confuse her by answering “normal.”

Others recommend candy or shouting “pomade.”

But nobody knows which rule works.

Perhaps there is no correct answer.

Perhaps Kuchisake-Onna lets some victims escape only so they will repeat the story.

So every child learns her question.
