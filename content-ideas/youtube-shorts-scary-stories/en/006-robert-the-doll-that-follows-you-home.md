# Robert — The Doll That Follows You Home

Inside a museum in Key West sits an old doll named Robert.

Beside his display are apology letters from around the world.

Visitors describe accidents, illness, and death after photographing Robert without permission.

The story began with a boy named Gene Otto.

At night, his parents heard two voices from his bedroom.

One belonged to Gene.

The other sounded deep and unfamiliar.

Whenever furniture moved or objects broke, Gene always said:

**“Robert did it.”**

Later, neighbors claimed the doll moved between the upstairs windows.

Today, Robert sits behind glass.

But cameras fail, his head reportedly changes position, and some visitors later see him in photographs taken inside their own homes.

That is why the museum's most important rule is simple:

Ask Robert before taking his picture.

Because every photograph may be an invitation.
