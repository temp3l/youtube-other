# The Forbidden Village Where Japan's Laws Do Not Apply

Deep in the mountains of Kyushu, Japan, there is said to be a village that appears on no map.

The only route supposedly passes through the old Inunaki Tunnel.

Near the entrance stands a warning:

**“Beyond this point, the Japanese Constitution no longer applies.”**

People who continue lose phone reception. Cars refuse to start. Screams and barking dogs echo from the tunnel.

But the worst part lies beyond a wall hidden in the forest.

There, a forgotten village is said to be occupied by a clan that accepts no outsiders.

Stories describe traps, an axe-wielding man, and a hut filled with bodies.

The legend would be easy to dismiss if a young man had not actually been murdered and burned alive inside the tunnel in 1988.

Since then, visitors have reported hearing a voice begging for help.

Maybe the village does not exist.

Or maybe nobody returns after finding the correct path.
